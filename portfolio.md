Assignment Week 6, Period 3 (16-03/20-03)

### 1. Write an essay of minimum 500 words about the importance of feedback, including at least the following topics:
- Why it’s important to be able to give proper feedback? Involve the 21st century skills also in your essay
- Why feedback is different from criticizing
- Mention the ground rules for proper feedback. Do not include the BECD-method in your answer, since this is an instrument for proper feedback, not a rule
- Describe an example of effective feedback you received in the (recent) past

I think that without feedback things become stagnant and don't change. This might sometimes work out if you are already doing well but that might not always be the case. Even if you are doing well, It can be a huge time and effectivity bonus to know that you are doing well and what parts are working well so you can keep doing those things. On the other hand if you are not doing well without feedback you first of all might not even know that you are not doing well. In this scenario you might just keep doing the same thing and eventually fail miserably. Personally I think that negative feedback is especially important. I like to think of it this way: if your ship is going in the right general direction positive feedback might just get you to your destination a little bit faster but if your ship is going in the wrong direction negative feedback is what will save you from crashing into the rocks. Despite this it might be shocking to hear that a lot of people don't like to give negative feedback just because of the word negative. People might be afraid of starting a conflict or hurting someone's feelings so they tend to just avoid the topic all together and look somewhere else. I know because, I hate to admit I too am still sometimes guilty of this behavior. I have been recently thinking about this and I recalled someone call this being the "allower". Thinking about it with that term suddenly makes it seem like you might be the wrong one in that sense and it might be some truth to it. You are not only deliberately refusing to offer someone potential benefit but you are also keeping yourself in a worse position than you could potentially be in. Now what can we do with the fear of insulting someone with our feedback. There is a method to formulate your feedback in a way that ensures that its constructive and not insulting. This is the BECD method which stands for Behavior, Effect, Change, Desired behavior. The idea is to:
- first describe what behavior you are commenting on 
- describe the effect of this behavior on you
- describe what consequences this behavior has on the environment 
- then give a suggestion on what to do about it
If you apply this template you can be confident that your feedback is not insulting and that it is constructive. However personally I think the key takeaway from this is to not just complain about something you don't like - criticizing, but also give a suggestion on how to improve it - feedback. This is what makes a feedback constructive - you construct something, not destructive - you only destroy something.
