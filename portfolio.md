### Do you think -after this theory class- that setting SMART goals actually helps in reaching important matters?

I would say that I don't think that it's super necessary to follow an exact template at every new goal but for me personally it is a good reality check to keep in mind the theory behind the SMART method. From experience I can say that goals that are according to the SMART method are more likely to be reached. Most importantly the Specific and Time-bound aspects. It is a lot more difficult to start working on a goal if I don't know what exactly I am working towards (Specific) and if there is no time urgency (Time-bound) I am more likely to work on other things that are more urgent. 

### Describe a situation where you set a goal for yourself and took actual steps to reach this goals. Elaborate please. Just realize: you all have done this at least once in your life

I remember when I wanted to learn how to flip a butterfly knife. To do this I found a set of tutorials, each bite sized focused on one specific trick. I carefully went through the instructions, first slowly and then I gradually went faster until I could do the trick without pausing. Doing this made me think that learning a new skill is a lot easier if you just start doing it and the way to ease the initial friction is to start with very small steps which can be completed individually. I realize that this is not entirely following the SMART method but I think it shows that taking even a few smart aspects can increase your chances.
